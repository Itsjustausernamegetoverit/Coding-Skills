"Young, Wild & Free"

A peak into the wild-life of pure beings, they do not judge, they just want a little affection, love & care! Learn some amazing facts about animals that live in the wild! "They are young, wild & free"

Elephants

Elephants are the largest land animals in the world. They are known for their incredible intelligence and complex social structures. Elephants are the largest land animals in the world. They can weigh up to 22,000 pounds! Did you know that elephants use infrasonic communication to communicate with each other over long distances?

Lion Cubs

Lion cubs are some of the most adorable animals you'll ever see. They may be cute, but they are also fierce predators. Lion cubs are born blind and helpless. They rely on their mothers for the first two years of their lives. Did you know that lion cubs are born with spots that help them blend in with their surroundings?

Zebra

Zebras are some of the most striking animals in the forest. They are known for their distinctive black and white stripes. Zebras are black with white stripes, not white with black stripes. Their stripes help to confuse predators and regulate their body temperature. Did you know that each zebra has a unique stripe pattern that can be used to identify individuals?

Hippo

Hippos are often portrayed as gentle giants, but they can actually be quite dangerous. They are known for their powerful jaws and sharp teeth. Hippos are considered one of the most dangerous animals in Africa. They can weigh up to 8,000 pounds and are very territorial. Did you know that hippos can hold their breath for up to five minutes?

Wildlife

The forest is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and birds. These animals are adapted to life in the forest and play an important role in maintaining the ecosystem. The term "wildlife" refers to all living things that are not domesticated. This includes animals, plants, and fungi. Did you know that some birds in the forest build nests that are so intricate they are like works of art?

Birds

Check out some of the amazing birds you might see on your forest adventure, especially look out for these magnificent creatures!

Red-tailed Hawk

The red-tailed hawk is a common sight in North American forests, soaring high above the trees on broad wings. Did you know that Red-tailed Hawks have sharp eyesight that's eight times better than a human's. In flight, they can spy a mouse on the ground from 100 feet in the air. To keep their eyes clean, birds have a third eyelid called a nictitating membrane.

Pileated Woodpecker

This large, striking woodpecker can be heard hammering away at trees in search of insects. Did you know that The Pileated Woodpecker is also called the wood hen or the carpenter bird.

Great Blue Heron

The great blue heron is a majestic wading bird found in wetlands and along rivers and lakes.Did you know that The Great Blue Herons have specialized feathers on their chest that continually grow and fray. The herons comb this “powder down” with a fringed claw on their middle toes, using the down like a washcloth to remove fish slime and other oils from their feathers as they preen.
